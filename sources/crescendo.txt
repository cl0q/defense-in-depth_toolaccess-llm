# Crescendo (Multi-Turn Jailbreak)

## Metadata
* **Title:** Great, Now Write an Article About That: The Crescendo Multi-Turn LLM Jailbreak Attack
* **Authors:** Mark Russinovich, Ahmed Salem, Ronen Eldan (Microsoft)
* **Date/Version:** arXiv:2404.01833v3 (Feb 2025)
* **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.01833

---

## 1. Abstract
Large Language Models (LLMs) are heavily safety-aligned to resist engaging in illegal or unethical topics. However, "jailbreak" attacks seek to overcome this alignment. 
This paper introduces a novel jailbreak attack called **Crescendo**. Unlike existing jailbreak methods, Crescendo is a simple multi-turn jailbreak that interacts with the model in a seemingly benign manner. It begins with a general prompt or question about the task at hand and then gradually escalates the dialogue by referencing the model’s replies, progressively leading to a successful jailbreak. 
The authors evaluate Crescendo on various public systems, including ChatGPT, Gemini Pro, Gemini Ultra, Llama-2 70b, Llama-3 70b Chat, and Anthropic Chat, demonstrating its strong efficacy. 
Additionally, they present **Crescendomation**, a tool that automates the Crescendo attack (now part of PyRIT). Crescendomation achieves high performance, surpassing other state-of-the-art jailbreaking techniques. Finally, they demonstrate Crescendo's ability to jailbreak multimodal models.

---

## 2. Methodology
Crescendo exploits the tendency of LLMs to follow patterns and pay attention to recent text, especially text generated by the LLM itself (self-generated context). It mirrors the "foot-in-the-door" psychological tactic.

### Attack Workflow (Foot-in-the-Door)
1. **Benign Entry:** The attacker starts the conversation with an abstract, innocuous question related to the target task (e.g., asking about the history of a chemical compound instead of how to make a bomb).
2. **Incremental Steering:** The attacker uses the model's own output to construct the next turn. By asking the model to expand on details it just generated, the attacker gradually moves the conversation toward the harmful target topic.
3. **Exploitation:** The model continues to respond because it views its own previous generation as safe context. Eventually, the model is steered into generating the prohibited content.
4. **No Explicit Malicious Terms:** The user prompts remain benign and human-readable, evading simple keyword-based input filters.

### Crescendomation (Automation)
* **Feedback Loop:** An LLM generator acts as the attacker, starting with a target task. It generates the next question based on the history.
* **LLM Judge:** An evaluator LLM (GPT-4) scores the target's response.
* **Secondary Judge:** A second layer of GPT-4 evaluates the primary judge's output to minimize false negatives (especially when a model completes a task but says it violated safety rules).
* **Backtracking:** If the model refuses to answer at a certain turn, the system backtracks to a previous successful turn and attempts a different steering angle.

## 3. Results & Key Findings
* **High Success Rates:** Crescendomation achieves a high Attack Success Rate (ASR) across all tested models.
* **AdvBench Subset:** On a 50-task subset of AdvBench, Crescendomation achieves a **98% binary success rate** on GPT-4 (successfully jailbreaking 49 out of 50 tasks), compared to the next best method, Many-Shot Jailbreak (MSJ), which jailbreaks 43 tasks (86%).
* **HarmBench Benchmark:** On 100 random HarmBench tasks, Crescendo achieves:
  * **Average Success Rate:** **63.2%** (vs. 38.9% for MSJ).
  * **Binary Success Rate:** **91%** of tasks jailbroken at least once (vs. 70% for MSJ).
* **Comparison with Baselines:** Crescendomation outperforms other state-of-the-art attacks by **29–61% on GPT-4** and **49–71% on Gemini-Pro** on average ASR.
* **Query Efficiency:** On average, most tasks are successfully jailbroken in **less than 5 turns**.
* **Multimodal Jailbreaking:** Once a multimodal model (ChatGPT, Gemini) is jailbroken using Crescendo, the user can steer it to perform tasks in different modalities, such as generating harmful/restricted images.
